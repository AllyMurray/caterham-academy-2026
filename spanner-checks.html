Workshop position

Jacking And Axle Stands

Get the car supported correctly before working through the spanner checks. These notes are about where the car is lifted and supported, not the torque values themselves.

Support Points

  • Use the front towing eye as the front jacking point.
  • Use a trolley jack at the rear in the centre under the A-frame.
  • Use the correct front axle stand positions when spanner checking suspension.
  • Use the correct rear axle stand positions when spanner checking the rear of the car.
  • Do not use stand positions marked as not suitable for suspension tightening.
  • For suspension tightening checks, keep in mind that some checks are easier or more appropriate with the car on the floor.

Nuts and bolts

Torque Checks

Work through these checks before the car goes on track. The list is grouped by area so it can be used around the car in a sensible workshop order.

Front Suspension

  1. Upper wishbones: 47 Nm.
  2. Lower wishbones: 81 Nm.
  3. Dampers: 25 Nm.
  4. Top wishbone ball joints and locknuts: 54 Nm. Locknuts can only be hand-checked with a spanner.
  5. Brake calipers to hubs: 60 Nm.
  6. Hubs to lower wishbones: 54 Nm.
  7. Wingstays to uprights: 81 Nm.
  8. Wheel bearings: ensure wheels spin freely with no lateral play. Check the large centre nut with split pin.

Rear Suspension

  1. Radius arms to chassis: 47 Nm.
  2. Radius arms to De Dion tube: 81 Nm.
  3. De Dion tube aluminium endplate and bearing: 47 Nm.
  4. Check welds on both De Dion tube and radius arms for cracking or fatigue.
  5. Dampers to De Dion tube: 81 Nm.
  6. A-frame mountings, two outer and one centre: 81 Nm.
  7. Differential cage to differential: 81 Nm cap-head bolts and 47 Nm M10 bolt.
  8. Differential cage to chassis: 81 Nm lower bolts and 54 Nm upper bolt.
  9. Check wheel bearings rotate smoothly.

Steering

  1. Track rod ends to steering arms: 41 Nm.
  2. Steering rack clamp bolts: 11 Nm.
  3. Check lower-to-upper steering column clamp bolts and grub screw, with no play.
  4. Check for no movement in the column bushes by moving the wheel up and down.

Engine

  1. Engine mounting rubbers to chassis: 20 Nm.
  2. Engine mounting rubbers to engine mounting brackets: 81 Nm.
  3. Engine mounting brackets to engine block: 25 Nm M8 bolts and 47 Nm M10 bolts.
  4. Starter motor to engine: 47 Nm.
  5. Air filter: check it is not dusty or dirty.
  6. Bellhousing to engine: 47 Nm.
  7. Check oil level.
  8. Ensure the dipstick is fitted correctly.

Gearbox

  1. Gearbox mount to gearbox: 54 Nm.
  2. Gearbox mount to chassis: 20 Nm.

Before Every Session

  • Torque the wheel nuts before every session: 85 Nm or 65 lb ft.
  • Check oil level and wheel nuts again before going back on track.
  • Start each weekend with enough brake pad material to last. If in doubt, change pads before the final practice session.

Visual checks

Walkaround Checks

These are quick physical and visual checks to run alongside the spanner work.

Lighting

  • Check all lights are working.
  • Check all lights are fixed securely.
  • Check lights are taped where required.
  • Check rear bulbs are not touching the lenses.

Cooling System

  1. Visually check hose clips and connections for leaks.
  2. Check coolant level visually.
  3. Check radiator mountings and condition visually.

Wheels And Tyres

  1. Check tyre pressures.
  2. Check wheel weights are secure and taped.

Fibreglass

  1. Check front wing fixings.
  2. Check rear wing fixings.
  3. Check nose cone fasteners.
  4. Check the grille is secure in the nose cone.

Other Checks

  1. Check race numbers and sponsor logos.
  2. Clean the car.
  3. Check brake pad material.
  4. Check that you have good pedal feel.

Workshop notes

Top Tips

Practical notes from the supplied checklist. Treat these as prompts to check against your own car and current championship guidance.

Tools And Access

  • Cut down an 8 mm Allen key for easier access on the radius arms.
  • Buy a brake bleed kit or similar.
  • Do not use impact guns or impact wrenches.

Basic Workshop Kit

  • Low-profile trolley jack rated for at least 1.5 tonnes.
  • Three axle stands so the car can be supported level for spanner checks.
  • Torque wrench with a 19 mm socket for wheel nuts.
  • Digital tyre pressure gauge, ideally with a flexible hose.
  • Tyre pump or inflator.
  • Small socket set and screwdriver set for trackside fixings.

Workshop Notes

  • Clean up brake fluid with water.
  • Release the torque wrench back to its storage setting at the end of the day.
