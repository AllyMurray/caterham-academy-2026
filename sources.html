Method

How The Site Was Cross-Checked

The text-heavy PDFs were read directly and the ARDS deck was also reviewed visually from rendered slide contact sheets, because automated OCR did not return reliable text from the slide images.

Source Priority

  • For technical legality, the 2026 regulations and later official bulletins override guide or presentation wording.
  • Where a presentation visual showed older values, the site keeps the current regulation values, such as 635 kg minimum weight.
  • Names, phone numbers, and similar contact details are kept as categories only, because they can become stale quickly.
  • Part-number links are practical buying references; always check current Caterham Parts supersession wording before ordering.

Confirm before acting

Open Questions

These remain deliberately visible because the PDFs identify them, but the current supply or fitting position should be confirmed before treating them as jobs.

Items To Confirm

  • Fuel sampling kit: the ARDS deck calls it out, but confirm whether it is supplied, fitted, or only required to be available.
  • Boot floor sealing: the ARDS deck shows this as a check item; confirm the reason and whether the factory now handles it.
  • Extinguisher visual checks: confirm the gauge, pin, and shipping seal before first use, then follow the pin-in/pin-out timing on the race prep page.
  • Superseded part numbers: if Caterham Parts lists a later suffix as a full supersession, order the current replacement but keep the regulation-listed number in the audit trail.
