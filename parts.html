Part Number Rule

  • Caterham may change part numbers during the life of the regulations.
  • A later suffix that fully supersedes an earlier suffix may be used where the earlier suffix is listed.
  • If a standard part is not identified in the regulations by a specific part number, the standard part is the part number in the car's bill of materials or a later bill of materials for an identical specification car.

Chassis / Safety

Permitted without a part number

  • Cut slots in the seat back aluminium panel for shoulder straps, with protection.

Permitted without a part number

  • Mandatory track prep Remove the front number plate during competition or track use for intercooler airflow; rear number plates may also be removed.
  • Use tape from the windscreen back to the front hoop of the roll cage to reduce rain ingress.
  • Use free-choice mirrors, subject to NCR visibility requirements.
  • For sprint only, temporarily remove external mirrors/sidescreens.
  • Remove passenger seat and passenger harness.
  • Modify the centre arm rest/tunnel top by removing padding and/or deforming the substrate for elbow room.
  • Use paint protection film on the leading edge of rear wings.
  • Run without sidescreens entirely, subject to mirror requirements.
  • Remove rubber trim and nut covers associated with IVA.
  • Use tape temporarily to secure damaged wings/nose cones at events.
  • Paint or vinyl-wrap the bodywork; foil wraps are allowed but may need reflection mitigation.
  • With scrutineer permission, paint/repaint/anodise visible components for presentation.
  • Adjust ride height subject to the minimum ground clearance rule.

Permitted without a part number

  • Free-choice ignition/starter switch, positioned on the dashboard.
  • Relocate or remove the standard fuel inertia cut-out switch, subject to loom/jumper rules above.
  • Fit a countdown timer or stopwatch to the dash or steering wheel centre.
  • Fit a non-integrated data-logging system and take a power feed and engine RPM signal.
  • Connect RaceSense/VBOX/scrutineer-approved similar datalogging to OBD/CAN for engine data.
  • Fit datalogging sensors such as throttle, brake and steering sensors, but not TPMS.
  • Use standalone sport video systems independent from the car except power feed.
  • Disconnect the wiring loom from the rear-wheel speed sensor.
  • When replacing a cycle wing, omit refitting the side repeater.

Fuel rules

Fuel

Required fuel source

  • Fuel rule Use commercially available pump fuel from normal forecourt pumps or circuit pumps.
  • Do not use fuel additives or illegal fuels.
  • The Driver Guide says the HORSE HR13 mapping was carried out on 95 RON regular unleaded, so super unleaded gives no performance advantage.

Tyres

Minimum weight

Weight / Ballast

Required minimum weight

  • Mandatory Minimum weight is 635 kg, including the driver in full racing kit.
  • Drivers weighing less than about 85 kg may need ballast to reach the minimum weight.

Permitted without a part number

  • Required if ballast fitted Add ballast if needed, fitted on the passenger side of the cockpit and mounted with at least four M10 fixings and suitable steel washers.
