Race weekends

Going Racing And Sprinting

Race weekends run to tight timetables. Prepare early and arrive with the car already ready.

Source: Driver Guide going-racing section and ARDS rules slides.

The Week Before

  • Clean cockpit, engine bay, suspension, wheels, and bodywork.
  • Spanner check the car and check brake pad wear.
  • Check sponsor decals, number backgrounds, race numbers, and scrutineering stickers.
  • If driving the race car to the event, cross out race numbers, remove tape from lights, inflate tyres 3-4 psi above normal pressure, and check lights.
  • If towing, check trailer tyres, wheel nuts, brakes, number plate, tie-down straps, and lights.
  • Pack the Caterham steering wheel, ignition key, and electrical cut-off key.

Documents And Kit Bag

  • Pack race licence and race upgrade card where required.
  • Pack race suit, underwear, balaclava, gloves, arm restraints, boots, helmet, and FHR.
  • Carry championship regulations and downloaded NCR Chapters 2, 5, 7, 9, and 12.
  • Carry circuit passes, final instructions, tickets, and the race-car registration document if road driving.
  • Take tools, tyre pump, pressure gauge, cleaning kit, jerry can, funnel, engine oil, coolant, brake fluid, and at least one spare wheel.

At The Event

  • Arrive early and park in the allocated paddock area.
  • Sign on as early as possible, online or in person as instructed.
  • If issued, take the scrutineering slip to the scrutineering bay.
  • For safety scrutineering, take suit, gloves, boots, helmet, and FHR as required.
  • Clean the car before scrutineering if it has been driven to the circuit.
  • Attend briefings, including first-time-driver briefings, and read any briefing notes.
  • Between scrutineering and qualifying or practice, check wheel nuts, tyre pressures, and fluid levels.
  • For qualifying, arrive at assembly early, fully dressed, belted correctly, and with arm restraints ready.
  • Complete three laps to qualify before pushing hard.
  • After each event, make notes and set a checking programme before the next meeting.

First competition

Sprint Guidance

The sprint is one car at a time, against the clock, with less traffic but the same need for preparation.

Source: Driver Guide sprint guidance.

Sprint Day Notes

  • Do as much car preparation as possible before travelling; the morning will disappear quickly.
  • Read all event paperwork, supplementary regulations, and final instructions.
  • Have documents, helmet, and overalls ready for scrutineering.
  • Bring enough fuel for the day, but remember that excess fuel adds weight.
  • Drink fluids and manage energy; the day is more tiring than it looks.
  • Listen for paddock announcements and be ready for runs early.
  • At the sprint start, green means the course is clear and you may go when ready. The clock starts when you break the timing beam, so do not creep.
  • Watch your 64 ft times as a guide to start quality.
  • Watch how other drivers start and approach the course.
  • Use the sprint to compete against yourself as well as others; analyse runs and look for time.
  • Ask questions early if unsure.

First race weekend

Testing, Timetable, And Race Sessions

The first race adds testing, qualifying traffic, starts, race procedure, and Parc Ferme discipline.

Source: Driver Guide first-race section and ARDS pre-session checks.

Testing

  • Testing is for competition licence holders in competition cars. Trackday overtaking rules do not apply.
  • Book pre-race test days early because places are limited.
  • Use testing to learn the track, try setup changes, and practise without treating the session as a race.
  • Be aware that faster and heavier cars may overtake aggressively under braking on open test days.

Race Weekend Discipline

  • Read final instructions. Each circuit can have different procedures.
  • Make a note of all relevant times and locations. Timetables can run ahead.
  • Do not be late. Missing qualifying can mean starting at the back or not racing.
  • For self-declaration and safety checks, check rain light, brake lights, windscreen cracks, handbrake cover, clear visor, extinguisher pin procedure, transponder LED, weight, ride height, helmet clearance, and numbers.
  • Qualifying is normally 15 minutes and races are normally 20 minutes, with boards for five minutes to go and last lap.
  • Watch mirrors and stay aware of other cars at all times.
  • Under a safety car, close the field safely into a compact line. When the safety car lights go out and it pulls in, the leader controls the pace and there is no overtaking until after the start line and green signals.
  • After qualifying or racing, go directly to Parc Ferme and stay with the car until released.
  • Only drivers, scrutineers, and authorised personnel should be in Parc Ferme unless permitted.

Rules on track

Rules, Flags, Protests, And Standards

The Driver Guide highlights these because they frequently catch new drivers out.

Source: Driver Guide rules section, NCR references, and ARDS rules slides.

Flags And Procedures

  • Blue flag: a faster car is approaching. Use mirrors and be predictable.
  • White flag: slow vehicle ahead.
  • Yellow flag: slow down, be prepared for danger, and do not overtake until after the green flag.
  • Yellow and red striped flag: change of surface conditions, such as oil, coolant, debris, or water.
  • Red flag: stop racing or qualifying immediately, but slow progressively, check mirrors, and signal if safe. In qualifying go to the pit lane unless instructed otherwise; in a race stop where marshals direct you and do not leave the circuit.
  • If a race is red-flagged, results or restart order may be counted back to the last completed timing point. If the red flag is on the first lap, expect the original grid to be reformed unless officials say otherwise.
  • Green flag: racing or overtaking resumes only after you pass the flag.
  • Black/white diagonal flag: driving standards warning if shown with your number.
  • Black/orange disc flag: mechanical issue; enter the pit lane and stop as instructed if shown with your number.
  • Black flag: enter the pit lane and stop as instructed if shown with your number. Do not assume a black flag is for you unless your number is displayed.
  • Chequered flag: the session is over; do not slow abruptly and do not pass it more than once.

Track Limits And Judicial Process

  • Stay on the track. A single wheel beyond the line or far side of a kerb can count as leaving the track.
  • Qualifying track-limits offences can delete the relevant lap time.
  • Race track-limits escalation in the Driver Guide is: first offence treated as misjudgement, second offence black/white flag, third offence five-second penalty, fourth offence drive-through, fifth offence exclusion.
  • If an incident needs protest, notify the Clerk within 30 minutes of results being published and follow the formal NCR process. Useful video evidence can matter.
  • If penalised, appeal through the Secretary of the Meeting within the permitted time and with the required fee. Check the current NCR for the fee amount.
  • Only the Clerk and officials can take formal action; telling Caterham personnel is not a substitute for the formal process.
  • If called to see the Clerk, use the championship handout or notes in the car to stay calm and follow the process.
  • Safe and courteous driving is the driver's responsibility. Racing is non-contact.
  • Driving Standards Improvement coaches are there to improve driving standards, not to help drivers win.
  • On trackdays, behave as an Academy ambassador. Do not use racecraft aggressively against regular trackday customers.
