Qualifying

Qualifying Tips

Think about qualifying rather than just driving as fast as possible for the whole session.

Source: Driver Guide qualifying advice.

Session Approach

  • Relax, get ready early, and avoid starting the session in a panic.
  • Know the cars and drivers slightly faster than you so you can learn from them if they appear ahead.
  • Let clearly faster cars past predictably; stay on line and signal awareness rather than jumping out of the way.
  • Find space. Do not turn qualifying into a race in a group of cars.
  • A fast lap starts at the last corner of the previous lap and ends only after crossing the line.
  • Consider weather: if rain is coming, early laps may matter; if it is drying, keep going.
  • Use the whole session. The first laps are banker laps to warm car, tyres, brakes, and driver.

Racecraft

Race Starts And Racing

Source: Driver Guide race-starts, overtaking, and defending sections.

Starts And Overtaking

  • After the green-flag lap, form on the grid, watch for the five-second board and red lights, then go when the lights go out. React to the lights, not the car ahead.
  • Use enough revs for a clean launch, but aim for only a small amount of wheelspin; left-foot clutch control matters most.
  • Do not try to win the race at the first corner. You normally have enough time to create safer opportunities.
  • Overtake where you can keep speed afterwards, not just where you can briefly out-brake someone.
  • Sometimes staying second until the final tow opportunity is smarter than passing too early.
  • Pressure the car ahead by being visible in mirrors, but do not drive unpredictably or weave.
  • Defend the inside line only when needed; defensive lines are slower and can invite an outside or cutback move.
  • If you make a mistake, stay focused and keep racing. Others will also make mistakes.

Driving

The Tow And General Driving Tips

Source: Driver Guide tow explanation and driving tips.

The Tow

  • The Caterham creates a useful low-pressure area behind it. The following car can gain speed in the tow on long full-throttle sections.
  • To use the tow, line up behind the car ahead, stay there until close, then pull out smoothly and firmly to pass.
  • Be ready for the tow to pull you into the car ahead if you misjudge closing speed.
  • To break a tow, the lead car can move once early, then return to the racing line if needed. Weaving is not allowed.

Driving Tips

  • Apply the brakes progressively; do not stamp on them.
  • Warm the tyres before driving quickly, especially the fronts.
  • Do not light up the rear tyres leaving the pit lane; it can create front/rear temperature imbalance and understeer.
  • Use steering inputs economically because every input costs speed.
  • At first, learn the track and your driving before blaming the car or chasing setup changes.
  • After gaining experience, experiment with tyre pressures, rake, geometry, and setup to suit your style.
  • Trackdays and tuition are useful practice, but testing and racing have different rules and traffic behaviour.
  • There is no substitute for time in the car, on road and track.
  • To finish first, first you have to finish.

Season knowledge

Marshalling, Contacts, And Mindset

Source: Driver Guide marshalling, contacts, and end-of-guide notes.

Marshalling

  • Marshals are volunteers and deserve respect and thanks. A wave at the end of sessions is appreciated.
  • After a race, whether you have won or not, acknowledge the marshals on the slowing-down lap.
  • Completing a marshalling day and getting the licence signature is worth ten championship points if done before the final event.
  • Do it early in the season if possible; it helps you understand how race meetings work from the other side.
  • Arrange marshalling through the championship contacts.

Contacts To Keep Handy

  • Championship assistants and Head of Motorsport for Academy matters.
  • Caterham Parts for permitted parts and paddock support parts.
  • Approved Caterham Motorsport Service Centres for Service Centre-only jobs.
  • HP Tyres for the mandatory Toyo Proxes CF2 tyre supply.
  • Trailer supplier contacts if using a trailer.
  • Use the latest official Driver Guide or championship communications for current names, phone numbers, and email addresses.

Mindset

  • Preparation is part of racing. Do not leave checks until the day before the next event.
  • Clean, presented cars are easier to inspect and less likely to be sent away by scrutineers.
  • Most early gains come from driver understanding, calmness, practice, and consistency rather than chasing a perfect setup.
