Setup

Race Setup Basics

Setup should be done in race trim, with the driver or equivalent driver weight in the seat and tyres at hot running pressures.

Source: Driver Guide race-setup section and 2026 regulations.

Main Setup Areas

  • The Academy car must run a front anti-roll bar. The orange and red options are chosen by preference, driver style, and conditions.
  • The thicker front anti-roll bar can shift grip balance toward rear traction but may unload the inside front tyre.
  • Set front ride height no lower than 5 mm above the 140 mm minimum at the regulation reference point.
  • Set rake after front ride height. Rear ride height is measured near the front edge of the rear wing; add the chosen rake amount to the front measurement.
  • Set the right rear spring seat about 5 mm higher than the left as a starting point to compensate for driver offset.
  • Set camber after ride height. Front camber is adjusted at the top ball joint; rear camber uses shims.
  • Start caster in the middle lower-wishbone rear mounting position, then adjust with experience.
  • Set tracking last because camber changes affect toe. Toe-out is normal on the front of an Academy car.

Flat floor

Flat-Floor Procedure

Source: Driver Guide flat-floor setup order.

Order Of Work

  1. Put the car in race trim.
  2. Put the driver in race trim, or add equivalent weight.
  3. Check scale pad evenness with the driver.
  4. Remove all fuel.
  5. Weigh the car.
  6. Add ballast if required.
  7. Refill with half race-fuel capacity.
  8. Set tyre pressures.
  9. Check and adjust caster.
  10. Check and adjust ride heights.
  11. Check rake.
  12. Check and adjust camber.
  13. Recheck ride heights.
  14. Set corner weights.
  15. Check and adjust tracking.
  16. Straighten the steering wheel.
  17. Check ride heights again and repeat as needed.

Between events

Maintenance And Checks

The Driver Guide repeats the spanner-check list because regular checking is what keeps the car reliable through race weekends.

Source: Driver Guide maintenance section and Spanner Checks PDF.

Core Maintenance Points

  • Use the Spanner checks page for torque values and physical nut-and-bolt checks.
  • Check front suspension, rear suspension, steering, engine, gearbox, wings, nose cone, lights, cooling, wheels, tyres, brake pads, and pedal feel.
  • The air filter usually needs no maintenance for the first 30,000 miles unless the car has run in dusty conditions.
  • Check oil with the engine warm, above 60 degrees C, then switched off. Wipe the dipstick, refit it, wait 20 seconds, then read it.
  • Add oil in small quantities, restart, run briefly, and recheck to avoid overfilling.
  • Keep oil at the maximum mark with the engine hot.
  • Make sure the dipstick is fully pushed home and secured so it cannot come out on track.
  • Check front wheel bearings for minimum play without being too tight.
  • Check rear brake pads regularly and inspect the inner pad carefully, as it is likely to wear first.
  • For extended track use, fit the approved Caterham competition front and rear pads listed in the regulations.
  • Keep the car clean enough that faults, leaks, loose parts, and scrutineering issues are easy to see.
