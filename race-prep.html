Driver safety

Fit And Wear The Safety Kit Correctly

These are not just build checks. They need to be right every time the car goes on track.

Harness, FHR, And Arm Restraints

  • Check the harness is routed correctly through the seat and around the FHR/HANS.
  • Check shoulder straps do not run upwards from the shoulder slots; FHR/HANS use should be no more than 20 degrees below horizontal.
  • Tighten the lap straps first, then shoulder straps, so the harness holds you low in the seat.
  • Wear arm restraints on track; do not just leave them fitted in the car.
  • Check arm restraints cannot catch on the handbrake, tunnel, or other cockpit fittings.
  • Confirm the seat-back void is filled and the seat foam/filler is still in place.
  • If a bead seat has been fitted or adjusted, confirm it does not restrict arm or leg movement.

Helmet And Cage

  • Confirm the delivery-prep helmet-to-roll-cage clearance check has been completed.
  • Before going on track, check the driver's helmet still has clear space to the roll cage when belted in.
  • Check roll cage padding is fitted wherever the driver can contact the cage.
  • Check roll cage padding is secure and has not moved during previous sessions.

Session prep

Before Going Out

These are the quick checks to complete before joining the assembly area or leaving the paddock for a session.

Car And Driver

  • Torque the wheel nuts to 85 Nm / 65 lb ft.
  • Check tyre pressures.
  • Check brake pad material and pedal feel.
  • Required visibility Check mirrors are fitted, secure, and provide the required rear visibility.
  • Mandatory Check tow straps are locked in position and accessible.
  • Check camera equipment is secure if fitted.
  • Check race numbers and sponsor decals are present and secure.
  • Check the fire extinguisher gauge is in the green and the bottle, pipework, and pull handle are secure.
  • Pull the fire extinguisher pin just before going out on track.

Lights And Tape

  • Apply headlight tape so it crosses the glass and can help retain broken glass.
  • Do not treat black tape as a problem by itself; the issue is whether the tape actually crosses the glass.
  • Tape restriction Avoid red and blue headlight tape.
  • Apply 50 mm gaffer tape from the windscreen back to the front hoop of the roll cage if needed to reduce rain ingress.
  • Check rear warning light, brake lights, and indicators are secure and working where required.

Paddock reset

When You Come Back In

Do the simple reset work immediately, while the session is fresh and before the next timetable pressure arrives.

After Session

  • Refit the fire extinguisher pin when you get back in.
  • Check tyres for pressure, wear, and pickup.
  • Check brake pad material and pedal feel again.
  • Check for obvious fluid leaks, loose panels, or damaged bodywork.
  • Review camera/data equipment while the session details are still fresh.

Event practicalities

Trackside Notes

Small preparation items that help between sessions and during a race weekend.

Useful Trackside Prep

  • Carry cable ties for the cycle wings in case one is lost or needs temporary securing.
  • Use Rain-X on the windscreen and visor, inside and outside.
  • Keep a short dash list for session reminders.
  • If the windscreen takes damage on the driver's side, plan how it will be rotated or replaced before further running.

Road use

Before Driving On The Road

Some event markings and competition setup details make sense on track but need changing before road use.

Road Reset

  • Cover or remove race numbers before driving the car on the road.
  • Refit the fire extinguisher pin before leaving the track environment.
  • Check lights, mirrors, and number plates are road-appropriate before road driving.

Scrutineering risks

Numbers That Can End A Result

These are worth treating as measured checks, not vague reminders.

Measured Checks

  • Mandatory Check ground clearance is at least 140 mm at the exposed lower chassis side-rail reference point, with the driver in full racing kit seated normally.
  • Mandatory Check minimum weight is at least 635 kg including the driver and full racing kit.
  • Required if ballast fitted If ballast is fitted, check it is on the passenger side and mounted with at least four M10 fixings and suitable steel washers.
  • If the driver is under about 85 kg, plan to check whether ballast is likely to be needed.
  • Check wheel centre caps are removed.
