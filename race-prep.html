Event admin

Paperwork, Kit, And Arrival Prep

The Driver Guide is clear that race weekends run to tight timetables. Treat paperwork, kit, fuel, and presentation as part of race prep, not background admin.

Week Before

  • Read the Supplementary Regulations and final instructions carefully and note briefing, sign-on, scrutineering, assembly, qualifying, and race times.
  • Attend mandatory briefings and listen for paddock announcements; race meeting timetables can run early.
  • Download or carry the championship regulations and the relevant NCR chapters, especially Chapters 2, 5, 7, 9, and 12.
  • Check circuit passes, entry tickets, paddock plan, and any car pass requirements.
  • Check championship sponsor decals, number backgrounds, race numbers, and scrutineering stickers are correct and secure.
  • Check decals are fitted to the supplied plan and replace any missing championship decals before scrutineering.
  • Clean the car: cockpit, engine bay, suspension, wheels, and bodywork.

Kit Bag And Documents

  • Pack race licence.
  • Pack upgrade card for race meetings where required.
  • Pack race suit, gloves, boots, underwear, balaclava, helmet, and FHR.
  • Check safety kit compliance against the Driver Guide safety kit notes.
  • Pack a clear visor if you normally use a tinted visor.
  • Pack arm restraints and make sure they are accessible before assembly.
  • Pack the car registration document if driving the race car on the road.
  • Take any scrutineering slip to the scrutineering bay if one is issued at sign-on.

If Towing

  • Check trailer tyre pressure and tread.
  • Check trailer wheel nuts, brakes, number plate, tie-down straps, and lights.
  • Pack the Caterham steering wheel, ignition key, and electrical cut-off key.

Driver safety

Fit And Wear The Safety Kit Correctly

These are not just build checks. They need to be right every time the car goes on track.

Harness, FHR, And Arm Restraints

  • Check the harness is routed correctly through the seat and around the FHR/HANS.
  • Check shoulder straps do not run upwards from the shoulder slots; FHR/HANS use should be no more than 20 degrees below horizontal.
  • Tighten the lap straps first, then shoulder straps, so the harness holds you low in the seat.
  • Wear arm restraints on track; do not just leave them fitted in the car.
  • Check arm restraints cannot catch on the handbrake, tunnel, or other cockpit fittings.
  • Confirm the seat-back void is filled and the seat foam/filler is still in place.
  • If a bead seat has been fitted or adjusted, confirm it does not restrict arm or leg movement.

Helmet And Cage

  • Confirm the delivery-prep helmet-to-roll-cage clearance check has been completed.
  • Before going on track, check the driver's helmet still has clear space to the roll cage when belted in.
  • Check roll cage padding is fitted wherever the driver can contact the cage.
  • Check roll cage padding is secure and has not moved during previous sessions.

Session prep

Before Going Out

These are the quick checks to complete before joining the assembly area or leaving the paddock for a session.

Car And Driver

  • Torque the wheel nuts to 85 Nm / 65 lb ft.
  • Check tyre pressures.
  • Check all fluid levels are correct.
  • Check brake pad material and pedal feel.
  • Required visibility Check mirrors are fitted, secure, and provide the required rear visibility.
  • Mandatory Check tow straps are locked in position and accessible.
  • Check camera equipment is secure if fitted.
  • Check the transponder LED is illuminated.
  • Check race numbers and sponsor decals are present and secure.
  • Check the fire extinguisher gauge is in the green and the bottle, pipework, and pull handle are secure.
  • Check the green plastic seal has been removed from the extinguisher pin so the pin can actually be pulled.
  • Pull the fire extinguisher pin just before going out on track.
  • Mandatory track prep Remove the front number plate before competition or track use for intercooler airflow.

Lights And Tape

  • NCR requirement Apply headlight tape so it crosses the glass and helps stop broken glass scattering into small pieces.
  • Electrical insulation tape generally works best. Black tape is fine; the issue is whether the tape actually crosses the glass and does the job.
  • Extend the tape about 50 mm onto the headlight bowl so it is clearly anchored, not just sitting on the glass.
  • Use diagonal "times sign" crosses rather than plus-sign crosses; they are usually easier to fit properly and look neater.
  • Tape restriction Do not use red headlight tape. Blue has also been called out in Academy guidance, so avoid red and blue.
  • Apply 50 mm gaffer tape from the windscreen back to the front hoop of the roll cage if needed to reduce rain ingress.
  • Check rear warning light, brake lights, and indicators are secure and working where required.

Paddock reset

When You Come Back In

Do the simple reset work immediately, while the session is fresh and before the next timetable pressure arrives.

After Session

  • Refit the fire extinguisher pin when you get back in.
  • Check tyres for pressure, wear, and pickup.
  • Check brake pad material and pedal feel again.
  • Check for obvious fluid leaks, loose panels, or damaged bodywork.
  • Review camera/data equipment while the session details are still fresh.
  • Put the extinguisher pin somewhere safe in the car when it is out, and refit it when not in use.

Event practicalities

Trackside Notes

Small preparation items that help between sessions and during a race weekend.

Useful Trackside Prep

  • Carry cable ties for the cycle wings in case one is lost or needs temporary securing.
  • Use Rain-X on the windscreen and visor, inside and outside.
  • Keep a short dash list for session reminders.
  • If the windscreen takes damage on the driver's side, plan how it will be rotated or replaced before further running.
  • Carry pump fuel in jerry cans; some circuits have closed or unavailable pumps, and sprint venues may not have fuel.
  • Carry a funnel or spout, engine oil, coolant, brake fluid, and at least one spare wheel.
  • Carry cleaning kit: bucket, sponge, car shampoo, leather, and polish.

Fuel Rules

  • Fuel rule Use commercially available pump fuel from normal forecourt pumps or circuit pumps.
  • Do not use illegal fuels or additives.
  • The Driver Guide says the HORSE HR13 mapping was carried out on 95 RON regular unleaded, so super unleaded gives no performance advantage.

Road use

Before Driving On The Road

Some event markings and competition setup details make sense on track but need changing before road use.

Road Reset

  • Cover or remove race numbers before driving the car on the road.
  • Remove tape from all lights before driving on the road.
  • If driving to the event, inflate tyres 3-4 psi above normal pressure and check they are road legal.
  • Refit the fire extinguisher pin before leaving the track environment.
  • Check all lights are working, especially rear fog and brake lights.
  • Check lights, mirrors, and number plates are road-appropriate before road driving.

Scrutineering risks

Numbers That Can End A Result

These are worth treating as measured checks, not vague reminders.

Measured Checks

  • Mandatory Check ground clearance is at least 140 mm at the exposed lower chassis side-rail reference point, with the driver in full racing kit seated normally.
  • Mandatory Check minimum weight is at least 635 kg including the driver and full racing kit.
  • Required if ballast fitted If ballast is fitted, check it is on the passenger side and mounted with at least four M10 fixings and suitable steel washers.
  • If the driver is under about 85 kg, plan to check whether ballast is likely to be needed.
  • Check wheel centre caps are removed.
  • Check the windscreen for cracks, especially in the driver's line of sight.
  • Check the car is clean and presentable before scrutineering; dirty cars may be sent away to be cleaned before a ticket is issued.
  • Do not present a wing held together with tape unless a scrutineer has already accepted it as a temporary event repair.
  • Check race numbers are proper numbers, not made from tape.
  • After qualifying or a race, go directly to Parc Ferme and stay with the car until released by the scrutineer.
