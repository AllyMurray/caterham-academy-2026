Harness

Harness Adjustment

Do the adjustment work before events, not in a panic at the first race morning.

Source: Driver Guide preparing-yourself section and ARDS harness demonstration slides.

Adjustment Notes

  • Pre-adjust crotch, lap, and shoulder straps before events.
  • Lap straps must be very tight and worn across the pelvis, not the lower stomach.
  • Crotch straps should be firm against the legs, routed up through the seat, across the inner thigh, through the D ring on the lap strap, and back towards the buckle.
  • HANS/FHR users should have the shoulder strap adjusters on the FHR yoke area and use the inner upper harness mounting positions.
  • If shoulder straps are too long, adjust the wrap behind the seat and lock the webbing correctly.
  • Roll excess webbing and secure it so it cannot flap.

In the car

Belting-In Order

Source: Driver Guide belting-in sequence and ARDS safety-equipment slides.

Sequence

  1. Place arm restraints on your lap or passenger side ready to use.
  2. Plug in and tighten the lap straps first.
  3. Feed the crotch straps through the seat and lap-strap D rings.
  4. Put each arm-restraint loop onto the relevant shoulder-strap clasp.
  5. Pass the shoulder strap through the crotch-strap loop and plug it into the buckle.
  6. Fit the arm restraints around the wrists, just above watch position.
  7. Tighten the shoulder straps firmly. If the lap straps are tight enough, the buckle should not climb up the chest.

Driver safety

Arm Restraint Checks

Source: Driver Guide arm-restraint guidance and ARDS arm-restraint slide.

Fit And Reach

  • Wear arm restraints on track; do not just leave them in the car.
  • Set them so fingertips can just touch the inside of the roll cage, but hands cannot pass the cage.
  • Make sure all controls and the centre mirror remain reachable.
  • The left-hand restraint is usually longer than the right. Mark the shorter right-hand strap.
  • Wear the loop on the wrist, not the forearm or upper arm.
  • Check they cannot catch on the handbrake, tunnel, or other cockpit fittings.
