Prep sequence

Start With The Factory Car, Then Prep After Delivery

The useful way to think about Academy prep is not "what can I change?" but "what should I order, and when should it be fitted?" The factory car is the baseline; the useful prep list is the set of post-handover parts, setup items, and checks to complete before testing.

1

Start From The Factory Handover Spec

Caterham handles IVA at the factory, so the car should arrive as a road-compliant car. Delivery prep starts after handover: keep competition-only changes separate from road-registration requirements, and use the full parts reference when you need to check a specific part number.

  • Treat anything that removes road-compliance equipment as post-handover prep.
  • Keep factory-supplied standard parts in the full parts list, not the shopping list, unless you need spares.

2

Order And Fit The Post-Handover Parts

The useful prep list is the set of permitted parts, setup items, and checks that make the car ready for testing. Order the parts that are not supplied by default, then fit them after the car has arrived in its factory handover spec.

  • Plan larger permitted parts such as the fuel cell route, passenger side impact bar, driver's foot panel, half sidescreens, mirrors, and front ARB before testing.
  • Fit the race master cylinder cap only as a post-handover race-prep change.
  • Only use Caterham or an appointed Caterham Motorsport Service Centre for immobiliser, switch, or fuel-pump wiring changes where the regulations require it.

3

Finish Driver Fit, Shakedown, And Hand Over To Race Prep

Before testing, sort the things that affect feel, reliability, and learning: driver position, brakes, data, and the setup baseline. Once the car is fitted and shaken down, use the race-prep and spanner-check pages for repeated event jobs.

  • Have permitted competition front and rear brake pads ready before the first proper test.
  • Plan shift-light, camera, and dash-timer placement early so wiring stays tidy.
  • Finalise seat, harness, FHR, steering, pedal, and helmet-clearance checks before setup work.
  • Use the race-prep and spanner-check pages for repeated event jobs, not one-off delivery prep.

Rules And Guardrails

  • Caterham may change part numbers during the life of the regulations.
  • A later suffix that fully supersedes an earlier suffix may be used where the earlier suffix is listed.
  • If a standard part is not identified in the regulations by a specific part number, the standard part is the part number in the car's bill of materials or a later bill of materials for an identical specification car.
  • Plan light removal, blanking covers, trim removal, or event-only bodywork changes separately from delivery prep.
  • Use permitted Toyo Proxes CF2 tyres sourced through HP Tyre Ltd, Daventry.
  • Avoid the specifically prohibited quicker ratio steering rack, part number 30S030A.
