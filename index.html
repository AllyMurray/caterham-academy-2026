Prep sequence

Start With The Factory Car, Then Prep After Delivery

The useful way to think about Academy prep is not "what can I change?" but "what should I order, and when should it be fitted?" The factory car is the baseline; the useful prep list is the set of post-handover parts, setup items, and checks to complete before testing.

1

Understand The Handover Spec

Caterham handles IVA at the factory, so the car should arrive as a road-compliant car. Treat that as the baseline. Delivery prep starts after handover, where the aim is to make permitted competition changes without confusing them with road-registration requirements.

  • Keep the factory IVA handover spec separate from competition-only changes.
  • Anything that removes road-compliance equipment should be treated as post-handover prep.
  • Use the full parts reference when you need to check a specific part number.

2

Order Parts For Later Fitment

Some permitted items are not supplied on the car by default. Treat them as parts to order separately, then plan the fitment after handover once the car has arrived in its factory IVA spec.

  • Order any larger permitted parts from Caterham Parts if you want them fitted for race use.
  • Plan later fitment for items such as the fuel cell route, passenger side impact bar, and driver's foot panel.
  • Order cockpit protection and visibility parts early enough that they are ready before testing.
  • Keep factory-supplied standard parts out of the shopping list unless you need spares.

3

Convert From Road Handover To Race Spec

After handover, the job is to make the competition changes that only make sense once the car no longer needs to remain in its road-handover form. The clearest example is the race master cylinder cap: it is permitted, but it removes the low-fluid warning.

  • Fit the race master cylinder cap only as a post-handover race-prep change.
  • Plan any light removal, blanking covers, trim removal, or event-only bodywork changes separately from delivery prep.
  • Confirm any immobiliser, switch, or fuel-pump wiring changes with Caterham or an appointed Caterham Motorsport Service Centre where required.

4

Prepare For Shakedown And Setup

Before testing, focus on the parts that affect feel, reliability, and learning. You want the driver position, brakes, data, and basic setup plan sorted before you start burning test time.

  • Have permitted competition front and rear brake pads ready before the first proper test.
  • Plan shift-light, camera, and dash-timer placement early so wiring stays tidy.
  • Confirm seat, harness, shoulder-strap slots, pedal position, steering wheel spacing, FHR suitability, and helmet-to-roll-cage clearance.
  • After any bead seat or seat-angle work, recheck shoulder belt routing over the FHR/HANS and make sure the seat does not restrict arm or leg movement.
  • Gather permitted setup parts before geometry, ride-height, and ARB work begins.

5

Hand Over To Race Prep

Once the car is fitted and shaken down, move the repeated event jobs to the race-prep checklist. Delivery prep should leave you with a known car, not a mixed list of one-off jobs and session reminders.

  • Use the race prep page for event, driver-safety, and scrutineering checks.
  • Use the spanner checks page for torque values, walkaround checks, and repeated physical inspections.
  • Use permitted Toyo Proxes CF2 tyres sourced through HP Tyre Ltd, Daventry.
  • Avoid the specifically prohibited quicker ratio steering rack, part number 30S030A.

Part Number Rule

  • Caterham may change part numbers during the life of the regulations.
  • A later suffix that fully supersedes an earlier suffix may be used where the earlier suffix is listed.
  • If a standard part is not identified in the regulations by a specific part number, the standard part is the part number in the car's bill of materials or a later bill of materials for an identical specification car.
