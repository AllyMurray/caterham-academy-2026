IVA caution

Leave Until After IVA

  • Do not fit the race master cylinder cap before IVA because it removes the low-fluid warning.
  • Keep road-registration requirements separate from race-event trim removal.
  • Confirm any switch, immobiliser, or fuel-pump wiring changes with Caterham or an appointed Caterham Motorsport Service Centre where required.

Testing

Prepare Before Shakedown

  • Have competition front and rear brake pads ready before the first proper test.
  • Plan data, shift-light, camera, and dashboard layout early so wiring stays tidy.
  • Confirm seat, harness, shoulder-strap slots, pedal position, steering wheel spacing, and FHR suitability.
  • Gather permitted setup parts before geometry, ride-height, and ARB work begins.

First event

Make It Scrutineering-Ready

  • Fit front and rear towing straps and the high intensity rear warning light with the correct clamp bracket.
  • Use permitted Toyo Proxes CF2 tyres sourced through HP Tyre Ltd, Daventry.
  • Carry common permitted bodywork spares: nylon rear wing screws, wingstay options, light blanking parts, and tape for temporary event repairs.
  • Avoid the specifically prohibited quicker ratio steering rack, part number 30S030A.

Part Number Rule

  • Caterham may change part numbers during the life of the regulations.
  • A later suffix that fully supersedes an earlier suffix may be used where the earlier suffix is listed.
  • If a standard part is not identified in the regulations by a specific part number, the standard part is the part number in the car's bill of materials or a later bill of materials for an identical specification car.
